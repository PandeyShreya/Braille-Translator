Learn Braille

Braille:
Braille is an essential mode of communication for the visually impaired. Braille is a code. It is a system of reading and writing a specific language without the use of sight. Braille enables people with blindness and visual impairments to read through touch. For many languages, including English, there are different versions, or “grades,” of braille. Braille is an important tool for educational advancement.

Braille Dots:
Braille codes are formed within units of space known as Braille cells. Six dot positions make up each braille character, or “cell”. These dot positions form a rectangle composed of 2 columns with 3 dots in each column. A single dot or any combination of dots may be raised at any of the six positions. Sixty-three combinations are possible using one or more dots, which are dots that can be used to represent an alphabet, number, number mark, punctuation mark, or even a whole word.


Dimension of Braille Cell:
  • Dot height: approx. 0.02 inches (0.5 mm)
  • Horizontal and vertical spacing between dot centers: 0.1 inches (2.5 mm)
  • Blank space between dots on adjacent cells: 0.15 inches (4 mm) horizontally and 0.2 inches (5.0 mm) vertically
A standard Braille page is 11 inches by 11.5 inches and typically has a maximum of 40 to 43 Braille cells per line and 25 lines.

Different Version of Braille:
  • Grade 1 Braille:
    • Grade 1 braille is a \underline{letter-for-letter substitution} of its printed counterpart. This is the preferred code for beginners because it allows people to become familiar with and recognize different aspects of the code while learning how to read braille. English grade 1 braille consists of the 26 standard letters of the alphabet as well as punctuation.
    Example: English Text: "dog and cat" Braille Text: "⠙⠕⠛ ⠁⠝⠙ ⠉⠁⠞"

  • Grade 2 Braille:
    • Grade 2, uses “contractions” that substitute shorter sequences for the full spelling of commonly occurring letter groups. The contractions are similar to English print contractions, like “cannot” versus “can’t”, in the way that a word is shortened. For example, “the” is usually a single character in braille. Grade 2 braille is the most commonly used form of braille code and is found in books, public signage, and restaurant menus, to name a few. It consists of the 26 standard letters of the alphabet, punctuation, and contractions.
    Example: English Text: "dog and cat" Braille Text: "⠙⠕⠛ ⠯ ⠉⠁⠞"

    The Braille Image to Text Converter is a cutting-edge web application that harnesses the power of machine learning and computer vision to convert Braille characters from images into readable text. With this intuitive tool, visually impaired individuals and Braille enthusiasts can effortlessly extract information from images containing Braille text and have it instantly translated into English. The main objective of this project is to bring awareness of Braille and to bridge the gap of communication between blind and non-blind people. As there were not enough technologies to help non-blind people interact and communicate with blind people. So, we created a braille-to-text converter that is able to take an image of a line (a word, sentence, or a character) of Braille, recognize it, and output its English counterpart. Using a specific ratio and cropping mechanism , we were able to take an image of a sentence/line of Braille and segment each Braille character out individually. We feed each of these characters into our model and then concatenate the output characters together into an English sentence or word.
